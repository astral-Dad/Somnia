Reality check techniques
✋
Hand check
Look at both hands. Count your fingers. In dreams, hands often appear with extra or missing fingers.
👃
Nose plug
Pinch your nose and try to breathe. In a dream, you can still breathe.
📖
Text check
Find text or a clock. Look away, then look back. Dream text is unstable and will change.
💡
Light switch
Try flipping a light switch. In most dreams, light levels don't respond normally.
🤜
Palm push
Try to push your index finger through your opposite palm. In a dream it often passes through.

FACT 01
Lucid dreaming was scientifically verified in 1975 by Dr. Keith Hearne, and later confirmed by Stephen LaBerge at Stanford using pre-agreed eye signal patterns during REM sleep.
FACT 02
During a lucid dream, the prefrontal cortex — the area responsible for self-awareness — becomes more active than in normal REM sleep.
FACT 03
REM sleep occurs in cycles of about 90 minutes. The most vivid dreams happen in the final REM period, typically 6–8 hours after falling asleep.
FACT 04
Approximately 55% of people have experienced at least one lucid dream, and around 23% report having them regularly.
FACT 05
Practicing reality checks during the day directly increases your chances of doing them in dreams. The habit transfers across the sleep boundary.

MILD — Mnemonic Induction
1. Set an alarm for 5–6 hours after sleep.
2. Wake, stay up 20–30 min recalling a recent dream.
3. As you return to sleep, repeat your intention mantra.
4. Visualize yourself becoming lucid in that dream scene.
Beginner friendly
WILD — Wake Initiated Lucid Dream
1. Wake after 5–6 hours of sleep.
2. Stay awake 30–60 minutes.
3. Return to bed and relax completely without moving.
4. Let the dream form around you while keeping consciousness.
Advanced
WBTB — Wake Back To Bed
1. Sleep for 5–6 hours normally.
2. Wake and stay up for 30–60 minutes.
3. Return to sleep — your mind is now primed for REM.
Beginner friendly
